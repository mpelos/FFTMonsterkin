[size=5]Monsterkin[/size]

[b]Reis' four Dragonkin support abilities now work on [i]every[/i] wild monster — not just Dragons and Hydras.[/b] Charm a Goblin into your party, hand a Chocobo an extra turn, buff a Behemoth before the fight of your life. They are renamed to match their new reach: [b]Monster's Charm[/b], [b]Monster's Gift[/b], [b]Monster's Might[/b] and [b]Monster's Speed[/b].

A tiny, [b]data-only[/b] mod for [b]FINAL FANTASY TACTICS – The Ivalice Chronicles[/b] (Steam, Enhanced). No EXE edits, no scripts — just one table field per monster job.

[size=4]The problem it fixes[/size]
In the vanilla game, four of Reis' abilities silently force a [b]MISS[/b] on any target that isn't a Dragon or Hydra — the classic "Dragon Check." That makes them almost useless: the moment your Dragoner tames a Dragon, those abilities have nothing left to do. Monsterkin opens them up to the whole bestiary.

[size=4]The abilities[/size]
[list]
[*][b]Monster's Charm[/b] (Dragon's Charm) — Charm a distant monster (100%). If it survives, it officially joins your party after the battle.
[*][b]Monster's Gift[/b] (Dragon's Gift) — Sacrifice your own HP to restore twice as much to a distant monster and cure its status ailments.
[*][b]Monster's Might[/b] (Dragon's Might) — Raise a distant monster's Bravery, Speed, and physical & magick attack power for the rest of the battle.
[*][b]Monster's Speed[/b] (Dragon's Speed) — Warp time so a distant monster's next turn comes immediately (Quick).
[/list]

[size=4]What's covered[/size]
[list]
[*][b]All 14 wild monster families[/b]: Chocobo, Goblin, Bomb, Panther, Squid, Skeleton, Ghoul, Ahriman, Aevis, Pig, Treant, Minotaur, Malboro and Behemoth — plus their special encounter variants.
[*][b]Dragons and Hydras[/b] still work exactly as before.
[*][b]Humans stay excluded[/b] — the species gate still applies to them, so nothing about your human roster changes.
[*]Story/unique monsters (Byblos, the Automaton, Lucavi, etc.) are intentionally left out.
[/list]

[size=4]Faithful to vanilla[/size]
[list]
[*]Same 100% rates, same effects, same status-cure rider on Monster's Gift. Only the [i]target[/i] restriction is widened.
[*]The three breath attacks and Holy Breath were never dragon-gated, so they are untouched.
[/list]

[size=4]How it works (for the curious)[/size]
The Dragon Check lives inside the game's (Denuvo-virtualized) formula code, so it can't be byte-patched — but it [i]reads data[/i]: each target's MonsterGraphic id from the job table (15 = Dragon family and 16 = Hydra family pass; everything else force-misses). Rendering resolves sprites through a completely different path and never reads this field in gameplay. Monsterkin simply sets MonsterGraphic = 15 for the 47 generic monster jobs via a small JobData.xml patch. One field per job; everything else inherits vanilla. Humans keep 0 and remain blocked. Confirmed against the PSX decompilation and proven in-game.

[size=4]Compatibility[/size]
Data-only and surgical: it touches a single field (MonsterGraphic) on the generic monster jobs and nothing else. It coexists with any mod that doesn't rewrite those same job rows. It does not alter human units, formulas, or sprites.
